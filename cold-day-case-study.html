Creative Technology Project

Project Continuum is an original intellectual property and development initiative created by Thisha Smith.

The project combines storytelling, business analysis, quality assurance, documentation, automation workflows, AI-assisted content production, and interactive media planning into a unified creative technology ecosystem.

The initiative serves as both a long-form narrative development environment and a practical demonstration of structured validation, process design, workflow management, and production governance.

Project Overview

Problem

Branching stories and interactive narrative systems can become difficult to manage when scripts, character continuity, scene logic, and production assets grow across multiple chapters or episodes.

Goal

Build a repeatable workflow for organizing story content, validating continuity, identifying inconsistencies, and supporting future game or visual novel production.

Outcome

Created a structured local production pipeline using documentation, automation planning, continuity checks, and AI-assisted workflow support.

Automation & Testing Concepts

Purpose: Converts story beats into structured image/video prompt concepts.

Purpose: Reviews tone, pacing, continuity, and cinematic consistency.

Regression Review

Purpose: Ensures new chapter edits do not break established canon or prior story logic.

Lessons Learned

Interactive storytelling requires the same quality discipline used in software systems: traceability, regression checks, clear acceptance criteria, and controlled change management. This project demonstrates how QA and business analysis methods can be applied to creative production pipelines to reduce inconsistency, preserve continuity, and prepare story content for future interactive formats.
